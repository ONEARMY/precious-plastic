beyond plastic

A Precious Plastic research project into the future beyond plastic. A future beyond waste. A future fuelled by bio materials that can decompose in nature. In any conditions. 

Beyond plastic journey

Research

We researched for months the world of bioplastics and its implications on the environment and waste management infrastructures.

Machine & moulds

We then designed, built and tested a machine to create biodegradable products that fully degrades in nature under any circumstances in a short time.

Material library

Finally we developed a material library with dozens of organic materials (like coffee ground, potato peel, orange and much more) to create biodegradable products.

Current bioplastics

More and more bioplastic products are making their way into the market. Claiming to be better. So people can keep consuming with peace of mind. However, the reality is a little different. The world of bioplastic is big and complex. A multi-billion industry that probably cares more about profit than biodegradability.

We threw bioplastics in nature for one year

To learn for ourselves about the biodegradability of so called bioplastics we did a little experiment. We packed a few bioplastic bags with different bioplastic single-use products and placed them in various environments for 18 months.

We placed one onto the roof for weathering by rain and sunlight, one into our compost, one got buried in a field, another one went into the canal next to the workspace and the last one in a big aquarium filled with ocean water. And after almost a year, we collected them to see what had happened.

To be fully honest the result was a little disappointing. Where in some places the very thin bags had dissolved slightly, most of the other products seemed basically untouched. Like what we would expect from conventional „non-bio”-plastics

Bioplastic vs biodegradable

Bioplastic is a bit of a complicated word, since it basically describes two different types of plastics.

One type are plastics made from biodegradable resources like corn, cassava or other organic matter. Those plastics can have the exact same properties and degradation times as conventional plastics. Meaning, you can make PP, PE, HDPE, etc. from biobased resources and call them bioplastic.
‍
The other type are plastics specifically engineered to biodegrade- to dissolve in nature under any circumstances in a matter of months.

A lot of these materials can biodegrade only if they are placed in specific conditions. Conditions not found in nature, but only in industrial composting facilities.

Say hello to the Bio Press

We designed, developed and tested a machine to help us create biodegradable products. We called it the Bio Press. It’s a fairly simple machine that uses heat and pressure to create three dimensional products from organic materials. And to make it applicable for different scenario it can be run manually or with the help of a compressor.

Various moulds

We developed various moulds to test the Bio Press and material library. You can find moulds for a sheet, bowl and cup in our master download kit.

Material library

You can use the Bio Press with pretty much any natural material you can think of. As long as it contains cellulose and starch to bind it together. Some materials work better than others and need different temperatures, times or additives. So to get you started we made a material library with many different recipes to make biodegradable products.
